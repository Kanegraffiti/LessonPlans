# Christ's Chapel Foundation School Midterm Exams
## Combined Exam: Basic Science, Basic Technology, and Information Technology
### Class: JSS3
Name: ____________________    Date: ____________________

**Objective Section (Answer all questions).**

1. A family trait passed from parents to children is called a(n) _____.
   a) habit  
   b) inherited characteristic  
   c) punishment  
   d) daily routine
2. A dominant trait is one that _____.
   a) appears only when both parents have it  
   b) shows up even if received from one parent  
   c) never appears in children  
   d) cannot be observed
3. Attached earlobes are an example of a ____ trait.
   a) dominant  
   b) recessive  
   c) neutral  
   d) inherited
4. Which feature is an example of a recessive trait?
   a) Dimples  
   b) Tongue rolling ability  
   c) Straight hairline  
   d) Freckles
5. The main purpose of treating timber is to _____.
   a) change its colour only  
   b) remove its bark  
   c) prevent decay and insect attack  
   d) make it heavier
6. A knot in wood caused by the base of a branch is a type of _____.
   a) grain  
   b) defect  
   c) finish  
   d) seasoning
7. Seasoning timber by stacking boards in open air is called _____.
   a) kiln drying  
   b) air seasoning  
   c) pressure treatment  
   d) lamination
8. A timber presentation where boards are cut parallel to the growth rings is known as _____.
   a) quarter sawn  
   b) plain sawn  
   c) rotary peeled  
   d) laminated
9. The main function of a router in a network is to _____.
   a) store user files  
   b) forward data packets between networks  
   c) print documents  
   d) supply electrical power
10. In computer networking, a hub _____.
    a) filters traffic intelligently  
    b) simply repeats signals to all ports  
    c) assigns IP addresses  
    d) encrypts wireless data
11. Which device allows multiple computers to share a single internet connection securely?
    a) Switch  
    b) Router  
    c) Printer  
    d) Speaker
12. The device that converts digital signals to analogue for transmission over telephone lines is a _____.
    a) monitor  
    b) modem  
    c) joystick  
    d) scanner
13. When both parents carry different genes for a trait and one masks the other, the hidden gene is _____.
    a) dominant  
    b) recessive  
    c) neutral  
    d) co-dominant
14. Which timber defect is caused by fungi due to improper seasoning?
    a) Warp  
    b) Blue stain  
    c) Knot  
    d) Shake
15. A safe way to protect wooden furniture from moisture is to _____.
    a) soak it in water monthly  
    b) apply varnish or paint regularly  
    c) leave it in the rain  
    d) place it directly on wet ground

**Theory Section (Answer 3 questions: choose 1 from Basic Science, 1 from Basic Technology, and 1 from Information Technology).**

1. (Basic Science) Explain how dominant and recessive traits differ, using two human examples and simple genetic terms.
2. (Basic Science) Discuss the importance of heredity studies in family health planning and give one practical example.
3. (Basic Technology) Describe two common defects found in timber and outline how each one can be reduced or prevented during processing.
4. (Basic Technology) Explain the purpose of seasoning timber and compare air seasoning with kiln drying.
5. (Information Technology) Illustrate how a small school network connects computers to the internet, naming the roles of the modem, router, and switch.
6. (Information Technology) State three ways to keep a home wireless network secure for students and teachers.

## Combined Exam: Basic Science, Basic Technology, and Information Technology
### Class: JSS1

**Objective Section (Answer all questions).**

1. Which statement best describes sanitation?
   a) Keeping only classrooms tidy  
   b) Keeping surroundings clean to prevent disease  
   c) Wearing clean uniforms alone  
   d) Burning all household waste indoors
2. A major reason for practising sanitation at home is to _____.
   a) increase dust  
   b) prevent the spread of germs  
   c) invite more pests  
   d) make floors slippery
3. Which tool is most suitable for packing refuse into a bin?
   a) Rake  
   b) Hoe  
   c) Dustpan  
   d) Cutlass
4. Foods that supply body-building nutrients belong to the ____ class.
   a) Carbohydrates  
   b) Fats and oils  
   c) Proteins  
   d) Vitamins
5. One example of a protective food is _____.
   a) Yam  
   b) Beans  
   c) Bread  
   d) Orange
6. Technology is best defined as the _____.
   a) study of living things  
   b) use of knowledge, tools, and skills to solve problems  
   c) act of playing games  
   d) punishment for wrong actions
7. Communication technology focuses on tools used for _____.
   a) planting crops  
   b) transporting goods  
   c) sharing information quickly  
   d) building houses
8. Which branch of technology includes machines that make goods in factories?
   a) Production/manufacturing technology  
   b) Transportation technology  
   c) Communication technology  
   d) Construction technology
9. The first help given to an injured person before reaching the hospital is called _____.
   a) rescue care  
   b) emergency surgery  
   c) first aid  
   d) therapy
10. Which item should always be in a first aid box?
    a) Textbook  
    b) Antiseptic solution  
    c) Stapler  
    d) Marker
11. Early humans first counted with _____.
    a) digital calculators  
    b) tally sticks and pebbles  
    c) smartphones  
    d) keyboards
12. The abacus is mainly used for _____.
    a) storing information  
    b) playing music  
    c) counting and simple arithmetic  
    d) drying clothes
13. Which device used marked pieces of wood to keep scores?
    a) Napier's bones  
    b) Tally sticks  
    c) Cowrie shells  
    d) Slide rule
14. The need for accurate counting led to the development of _____.
    a) slower transport  
    b) mechanical and electronic computers  
    c) paper maps  
    d) chalkboards
15. In early trading, cowrie shells were used mainly for _____.
    a) decoration only  
    b) counting and recording value  
    c) music production  
    d) lighting fires

**Theory Section (Answer 3 questions: choose 1 from Basic Science, 1 from Basic Technology, and 1 from Information Technology).**

1. (Basic Science) Explain three personal hygiene practices that help prevent the spread of diseases at home and school.
2. (Basic Science) Describe the steps for cleaning and proper disposal of refuse in a school compound.
3. (Basic Technology) List four branches of technology and give one example for each branch.
4. (Basic Technology) State three reasons why first aid knowledge is important for pupils and mention two items found in a first aid box.
5. (Information Technology) Trace the development of counting devices from tally sticks to the abacus, highlighting one limitation of each stage.
6. (Information Technology) Describe two situations in which early counting tools were used and explain why accuracy was important.

SECOND SEMESTER LESSON PLAN – JSS1 BASIC SCIENCE (WEEKS 1–8)

WEEK 1
Date: __________________
Class: JSS1 (Basic 7)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 1st Period
Topic: Living and Non-living Things
Subtopic: Characteristics of living things
Reference Materials:
- NERDC Basic Science Curriculum (Upper Basic)
- State Scheme of Work (Second Term)
Instructional Materials:
- Chart of plants, animals, stones, and water
- Whiteboard and marker
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners can identify common objects in their environment.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners mention things found at home and school.
Behavioural Objectives:
- Define living and non-living things.
- List five characteristics of living things.
Lesson Presentations:
- Teacher displays pictures and guides classification.
- Learners group examples into living/non-living.
Lesson Content:
- Living things move, feed, respire, grow, reproduce, excrete, and respond to stimuli.
- Non-living things do not perform life processes.
Evaluation:
- State four characteristics of living things.
Conclusion:
- Living things carry out life processes while non-living things do not.
Assignment:
- Draw two living and two non-living things.

WEEK 2
Date: __________________
Class: JSS1 (Basic 7)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 2nd Period
Topic: Classification of Plants and Animals
Subtopic: Grouping by observable features
Reference Materials:
- NERDC Basic Science Curriculum (Upper Basic)
- Approved Basic Science Textbook
Instructional Materials:
- Flash cards of vertebrates/invertebrates and flowering/non-flowering plants
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners know examples of plants and animals.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners mention birds, fish, grasses, and trees.
Behavioural Objectives:
- Classify animals into vertebrates and invertebrates.
- Classify plants into flowering and non-flowering plants.
Lesson Presentations:
- Teacher explains simple classification rules.
- Learners sort flash cards in groups.
Lesson Content:
- Vertebrates have backbone; invertebrates do not.
- Flowering plants produce flowers/seeds; non-flowering plants include ferns/mosses.
Evaluation:
- Give two examples each of vertebrates and invertebrates.
Conclusion:
- Classification helps us study organisms better.
Assignment:
- List five plants around your home and state if flowering or non-flowering.

WEEK 3
Date: __________________
Class: JSS1 (Basic 7)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 3rd Period
Topic: States of Matter
Subtopic: Solids, liquids, and gases
Reference Materials:
- NERDC Basic Science Curriculum (Upper Basic)
Instructional Materials:
- Water, stone, balloon with air
- Chart showing particle arrangement
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners have handled water and solid objects.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners identify examples of solid, liquid, gas.
Behavioural Objectives:
- Describe three states of matter.
- Give three examples for each state.
Lesson Presentations:
- Teacher demonstrates with materials.
- Learners identify particle spacing.
Lesson Content:
- Solids have fixed shape and volume.
- Liquids have fixed volume but no fixed shape.
- Gases have no fixed shape and no fixed volume.
Diagram:
[Solid]  ●●●●
         ●●●●
[Liquid] ● ●● ●
         ●  ● ●
[Gas]    ●     ●    ●
            ●      ●
Evaluation:
- Differentiate between solids and liquids.
Conclusion:
- Matter exists in three common states.
Assignment:
- Draw and label one example each of solid, liquid, and gas.

WEEK 4
Date: __________________
Class: JSS1 (Basic 7)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 4th Period
Topic: Changes of State
Subtopic: Melting, freezing, evaporation, condensation
Reference Materials:
- Approved Basic Science Textbook
Instructional Materials:
- Ice, kettle image, water cycle chart
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners know states of matter.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners have seen ice melt.
Behavioural Objectives:
- Explain four processes of state change.
- Relate changes of state to daily life.
Lesson Presentations:
- Teacher explains and demonstrates examples.
- Learners discuss examples at home.
Lesson Content:
- Melting: solid to liquid.
- Freezing: liquid to solid.
- Evaporation: liquid to gas.
- Condensation: gas to liquid.
Diagram:
Solid --(heating)--> Liquid --(heating)--> Gas
Solid <--(cooling)-- Liquid <--(cooling)-- Gas
Evaluation:
- What is condensation?
Conclusion:
- Heating and cooling cause state changes.
Assignment:
- Write two examples of evaporation in daily life.

WEEK 8
Date: __________________
Class: JSS1 (Basic 7)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 8th Period
Topic: Disease Prevention
Subtopic: Water-borne diseases and hygiene
Reference Materials:
- NERDC Curriculum
- School health guide
Instructional Materials:
- Hand-washing poster
- Water treatment chart
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners have discussed clean water.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners mention common illnesses in the community.
Behavioural Objectives:
- Define water-borne diseases.
- Mention prevention methods.
Lesson Presentations:
- Teacher discusses cholera, typhoid, dysentery.
- Learners role-play correct hand-washing.
Lesson Content:
- Water-borne diseases spread through contaminated water.
- Prevention: boiling, filtration, sanitation, hand-washing, proper waste disposal.
Diagram:
Dirty Water -> Germs -> Drinking -> Illness
Clean Water + Hygiene -> Healthy Body
Evaluation:
- Mention three ways to prevent water-borne diseases.
Conclusion:
- Good hygiene and safe water reduce disease spread.
Assignment:
- Write five personal hygiene rules practiced at home.

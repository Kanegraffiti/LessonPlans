DETAILED LESSON PLAN – JSS 1
-----------------------------

- **Date:** Monday, 15th September 2025
- **Class:** JSS 1
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Period:** First
- **Topic:** Historical Development of Computers
- **Subtopic:** Early Counting Devices
- **Reference Materials:** NERDC Basic Science and Technology for JSS 1; Illustrated Computer Studies Textbook for JSS 1
- **Instructional Materials:** Pictures of counting devices (fingers, stones, tally sticks, abacus), real or improvised abacus, number charts
- **Entry Behaviour:** Pupils have used their fingers and everyday objects to count small quantities.
- **Behavioural Objectives:** By the end of the lesson, pupils should be able to:
  1. Explain why early humans needed counting devices.
  2. Identify at least four early counting devices used before modern computers.
  3. Describe how selected early counting devices work.
  4. Demonstrate counting with an abacus or improvised counting device.
  5. Arrange early counting devices in chronological order during a class activity.

### Lesson Presentation

**Step 1 – Introduction:** Teacher asks pupils how they keep track of items when shopping in the market and lists their methods on the board.

**Step 2 – Presentation:** Teacher explains that before modern computers, people used simple tools such as fingers, stones, and tally sticks to count and keep records.

**Step 3 – Explanation:** Teacher displays pictures of early counting devices, describes how each was used, and emphasises the introduction of the abacus as a major improvement.

**Step 4 – Application:** Pupils work in groups to practise counting with an abacus or improvised counters and present their results.

**Step 5 – Evaluation:** Teacher asks pupils to mention three early counting devices, describe one, and state why counting devices were important to early humans.

### Content Summary

1. Early humans used fingers, stones, and sticks to count and record items.
2. The abacus was developed to make counting faster and more accurate.
3. Counting devices helped people trade, plan, and solve daily number problems.

### Conclusion

Teacher reviews the early counting devices discussed and links them to the development of modern computers.

### Assignment

Draw and label three early counting devices and state one use for each in your notebooks.


DETAILED LESSON PLAN – JSS 2
-----------------------------

- **Date:** Wednesday, 17th September 2025
- **Class:** JSS 2
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Period:** Second
- **Topic:** Computer Software
- **Subtopic:** Introduction and Definition of Software
- **Reference Materials:** NERDC Computer Studies for JSS 2; Comprehensive Computer Studies Textbook for Junior Secondary Schools Book 2
- **Instructional Materials:** Computer system, projector slides showing software icons, software packaging, flashcards of software types
- **Entry Behaviour:** Students have used computers for typing and playing educational games.
- **Behavioural Objectives:** By the end of the lesson, students should be able to:
  1. Define software correctly.
  2. Differentiate between hardware and software with examples.
  3. State the major categories of software.
  4. Identify examples of application and system software from icons or packaging.
  5. Explain why software is essential for running computer hardware.

### Lesson Presentation

**Step 1 – Introduction:** Teacher asks students to list what they see on the computer screen when it is turned on and guides discussion on programs they have used.

**Step 2 – Presentation:** Teacher defines software as the set of instructions or programs that tell the computer what to do.

**Step 3 – Explanation:** Teacher explains the difference between hardware and software and introduces categories such as system software and application software.

**Step 4 – Application:** Students examine software icons or packages, classify them into system or application software, and justify their choices in groups.

**Step 5 – Evaluation:** Teacher asks students to define software, differentiate it from hardware, and name one example each of system and application software.

### Content Summary

1. Software is the set of programs or instructions that direct computer operations.
2. Hardware refers to the physical parts of the computer that can be touched.
3. Software categories include system software (e.g. operating systems) and application software (e.g. word processors).

### Conclusion

Teacher summarises the definition and categories of software and emphasises the relationship between hardware and software.

### Assignment

List five software applications you use and state whether each is system or application software.


DETAILED LESSON PLAN – JSS 3
-----------------------------

- **Date:** Friday, 19th September 2025
- **Class:** JSS 3
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Period:** Third
- **Topic:** Computer Career Opportunities
- **Subtopic:** Computer Professionals and Their Functions
- **Reference Materials:** NERDC Computer Studies for JSS 3; Careers in ICT Textbook for Junior Secondary Schools Book 3
- **Instructional Materials:** Charts of computer professions, career profiles, video clips on ICT careers, interview excerpts from professionals
- **Entry Behaviour:** Students are familiar with common ICT tools and have seen professionals using computers.
- **Behavioural Objectives:** By the end of the lesson, students should be able to:
  1. List different computer-related professions.
  2. Describe the primary functions of each highlighted computer professional.
  3. Match computer professionals with the tools or software they use.
  4. Explain the importance of computer professionals in society.
  5. Identify computer career opportunities that align with their personal interests.

### Lesson Presentation

**Step 1 – Introduction:** Teacher asks students to mention people they know who work with computers and what they do.

**Step 2 – Presentation:** Teacher lists computer professionals such as system analysts, programmers, network administrators, web developers, database administrators, and ICT teachers.

**Step 3 – Explanation:** Teacher describes the functions of each professional, highlighting tasks like designing systems, writing programs, managing networks, and teaching ICT.

**Step 4 – Application:** Students work in groups to match career cards to job descriptions and present the functions of each professional to the class.

**Step 5 – Evaluation:** Teacher assesses students through oral questions requiring them to name a computer professional, describe their function, and state tools they use.

### Content Summary

1. Computer careers include system analysts, programmers, network administrators, database administrators, web developers, ICT support staff, and ICT teachers.
2. Functions: system analysts study organisational needs and design solutions; programmers write code; network administrators manage network resources; database administrators ensure data security; web developers design and maintain websites; ICT teachers train users.
3. Computer professionals support businesses, education, health, government, and entertainment sectors.

### Conclusion

Teacher recaps the computer professions discussed and encourages students to explore ICT career pathways.

### Assignment

Choose one computer professional discussed in class, research additional responsibilities, and write a short paragraph on why the role is important.

WEEK 1

### Contact 1
- **Date:** ____________________
- **Class:** JSS 3
- **Duration:** 40 minutes
- **Period:** __________
- **Subject:** Basic Technology
- **Topic:** Isometric Drawing
- **Subtopic:** Meaning and examples of isometric drawing
- **Reference Materials:** NERDC Basic Technology Curriculum (JSS 3); Basic Technology Textbook (JSS 3)
- **Instructional Materials:** Isometric grid charts, sample isometric drawings, ruler, set square, pencil
- **Previous Knowledge:** Students have drawn basic 2D shapes with instruments.
- **Entry Behaviour:** Students can identify simple geometric shapes.
- **Behavioural Objectives:** By the end of the lesson, students should be able to:
  1. Define isometric drawing.
  2. Identify at least three examples of isometric drawings.
  3. Describe features of isometric drawings (equal angles, equal lengths).
- **Lesson Presentations:**
  - **Introduction:** Display a cube and a box; ask how they can be drawn to show three faces.
  - **Presentation:** Define isometric drawing and show examples.
  - **Explanation:** Explain isometric axes and 120° angles between axes.
  - **Student Activities:** Students observe and label features on sample drawings.
  - **Teacher Activities:** Guide discussion and correct misconceptions.
- **Lesson Content:** Meaning of isometric drawing; examples (cube, rectangular block, stepped block).
- **Evaluation:** Students define isometric drawing and list three examples.
- **Conclusion:** Summarize key features of isometric drawing.
- **Assignment:** List three objects at home that could be drawn in isometric form.

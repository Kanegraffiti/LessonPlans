==================================================================

SCHEME OF WORK OVERVIEW
------------------------
Week 1: Resources from Living Things I (Meaning of resources; plant resources)
Week 2: Resources from Living Things II (Resources from animals)
Week 3: Economic Importance of Living Resources
Week 4: Resources from Non-Living Things I (Solid Minerals)
Week 5: Resources from Non-Living Things II (Location in Nigeria)

WEEK 1: RESOURCES FROM LIVING THINGS I
-------------------------------------

### Contact 1
- **Date:**
- **Class:** JSS 3
- **Subject:** Basic Science & Technology
- **Duration:** 40 minutes
- **Period:**
- **Topic:** Resources from Living Things I
- **Subtopic:** Meaning of resources
- **Reference Materials:** NERDC Basic Science & Technology for JSS 3; approved JSS 3 Basic Science text
- **Instructional Materials:** Chart showing types of resources, flashcards, pictures of plant products
- **Previous Knowledge:** Learners can mention items used in daily life (food, clothes, tools).
- **Entry Behaviour:** Learners have seen farm produce and plant products in the market.
- **Behavioural Objectives:** By the end of the lesson, learners should be able to:
  1. Define resources in their own words.
  2. Distinguish living resources from non-living resources.
  3. Give two examples of resources from living things.
  4. Explain why resources are useful to people.
- **Lesson Presentations:**
  - **Step 1 (Introduction):** Ask learners to mention items they use daily and classify them as from living or non-living sources.
  - **Step 2 (Presentation):** Define resources and explain that living resources come from plants and animals.
  - **Step 3 (Explanation):** Discuss examples of living resources and their everyday uses.
  - **Step 4 (Activity):** Learners work in pairs to list five resources from living things.
  - **Step 5 (Evaluation):** Ask learners to define resources and give two examples from living things.
- **Lesson Content:**
  - Resources are useful materials obtained from the environment.
  - Living resources come from plants and animals.
  - Examples: food crops, timber, meat, milk.
- **Evaluation:** Define resources and list two living resources.
- **Conclusion:** Summarise the meaning of resources and the idea of living resources.
- **Assignment:** Write three items used at home that come from living things.

- **Class:** JSS 3

- **Period:**
- **Topic:** Light Energy I
- **Subtopic:** Reflection of light
- **Reference Materials:** NERDC Basic Science & Technology for JSS 3; approved JSS 3 Basic Science text
- **Instructional Materials:** Plane mirror, torchlight, white screen
- **Previous Knowledge:** Learners know sources of light.
- **Entry Behaviour:** Learners have used mirrors at home.

  1. Define reflection of light.

  - **Step 1 (Introduction):** Shine a torchlight on a mirror and observe the reflected ray.

  - **Step 4 (Activity):** Learners draw a simple reflection diagram.
  - **Step 5 (Evaluation):** Ask learners to define reflection and state the law.
- **Lesson Content:**
  - Reflection is the bouncing back of light from a surface.
  - Angle of incidence equals angle of reflection.
- **Evaluation:** Explain reflection with an example.
- **Conclusion:** Summarise the meaning of reflection and the law.
- **Assignment:** Draw and label a reflection diagram with a mirror.

CONTACT 1
Date: __________________
Class: JSS1 (Basic 7)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 1st Period
Subject: Computer Studies
Topic: Computer and Monitor
Subtopic: Meaning and functions of monitor
Reference Materials:
- NERDC Computer Studies Curriculum for Upper Basic Education (JSS1)
- State Scheme of Work for Second Term Computer Studies
- Approved Computer Studies textbooks for Junior Secondary Schools
Instructional Materials:
- Desktop/laptop computer
- Monitor picture chart
- Whiteboard and marker
- Real monitor (CRT/LCD where available)
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners know basic parts of computer system.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners can identify keyboard and mouse.
Behavioural Objectives:
By the end of the lesson, learners should be able to:
- define monitor.
- state at least three functions of monitor.
- identify monitor as an output device.
- list two major categories of monitor.
- mention differences between monochrome and colour monitor.
Lesson Presentations:
Step 1: Introduction/Set Induction
- Teacher switches on a monitor and asks learners what they can see.
- Learners discuss what happens when no monitor is connected.
Step 2: Presentation/Teaching Steps
- Teacher defines monitor and explains output device concept.
- Teacher states monitor functions in displaying text, graphics, and video.
- Teacher introduces categories of monitor: monochrome and colour.
- Teacher explains display quality in simple terms.
Step 3: Learners’ Activities
- Learners identify monitor from computer setup.
- Learners list functions in pairs.
- Learners answer guided questions.
Step 4: Summary/Board Summary
- Definition of monitor.
- Functions and category types.
- Importance in computer use.
Lesson Content:
Monitor
- A monitor is an output device that displays information from the computer.

Functions of Monitor
- Displays text and numbers.
- Displays pictures and graphics.
- Displays video and animations.
- Shows user interface for interaction.

Categories
- Monochrome monitor: displays one colour shade.
- Colour monitor: displays multiple colours.
Evaluation:
1. Define monitor.
2. Mention three functions of monitor.
3. Why is monitor called an output device?
4. List two categories of monitor.
5. Differentiate monochrome and colour monitor.
6. State one importance of monitor in classroom computer use.
Conclusion:
- Teacher concludes that monitor is essential for viewing and using computer output effectively.
Assignment:
1. Write and explain the meaning and functions of monitor.
2. Draw and label a monitor as part of a computer set.
3. List four activities that require monitor display.

CONTACT 2
Date: __________________
Class: JSS1 (Basic 7)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 2nd Period
Subject: Computer Studies
Topic: Computer and Monitor
Subtopic: Types of monitors and display adapters
Reference Materials:
- NERDC Computer Studies Curriculum for Upper Basic Education (JSS1)
- State Scheme of Work for Second Term Computer Studies
- Approved Computer Studies textbooks for Junior Secondary Schools
Instructional Materials:
- Charts showing CRT and LCD monitors
- Flash cards with adapter names (CGA, EGA, VGA, SVGA)
- Whiteboard and marker
- Computer setup for demonstration
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners can define monitor and mention its functions.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners can identify monitor in a computer lab.
Behavioural Objectives:
By the end of the lesson, learners should be able to:
- identify CRT and LCD monitor types.
- state at least two features of CRT monitor.
- state at least two features of LCD monitor.
- list common display adapters and full meanings.
- compare CRT and LCD monitors.
Lesson Presentations:
Step 1: Introduction/Set Induction
- Teacher revises Contact 1 with oral drill.
- Teacher displays pictures of CRT and LCD for comparison.
Step 2: Presentation/Teaching Steps
- Teacher explains CRT and LCD monitor technologies at basic level.
- Teacher discusses differences in size, weight, and power use.
- Teacher introduces display adapters: CGA, EGA, VGA, SVGA.
- Teacher gives simple interpretation of display quality progression.
Step 3: Learners’ Activities
- Learners identify monitor type from displayed images.
- Learners complete matching exercise: adapter acronym to full meaning.
- Learners discuss advantages of LCD over CRT.
Step 4: Summary/Board Summary
- CRT and LCD features.
- Adapter names and meanings.
- Basic comparison points.
Lesson Content:
Types of Monitors
- CRT (Cathode Ray Tube): bulky, heavier, older technology.
- LCD (Liquid Crystal Display): flat, lighter, lower power consumption.

Display Adapters
- CGA: Colour Graphics Adapter.
- EGA: Enhanced Graphics Adapter.
- VGA: Video Graphics Adapter.
- SVGA: Super Video Graphics Adapter.

Comparison
- CRT occupies more space; LCD saves space.
- LCD generally gives better convenience and lower power use.
Evaluation:
1. Give the full meaning of CRT and LCD.
2. Mention two features of CRT monitor.
3. Mention two features of LCD monitor.
4. Expand CGA, EGA, VGA, and SVGA.
5. Which monitor type is more space-saving and why?
6. State one reason schools prefer LCD monitors today.
Conclusion:
- Teacher concludes that monitor type and adapter quality affect display performance and user convenience.
Assignment:
1. Write and explain differences between CRT and LCD monitors.
2. Draw and label CRT and LCD monitors.
3. List and explain the full meaning of CGA, EGA, VGA, and SVGA.
4. Suggest monitor type for a modern school ICT lab with reasons.

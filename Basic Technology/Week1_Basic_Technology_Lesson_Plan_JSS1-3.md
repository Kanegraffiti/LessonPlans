DETAILED LESSON PLAN – JSS 1
-----------------------------

- **Date:** Monday, 15th September 2025
- **Class:** JSS 1
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Period:** First
- **Topic:** Introduction to Technology
- **Subtopic:** Meaning, Importance, and Branches of Technology
- **Reference Materials:** NERDC Basic Science and Technology for JSS 1; MAN Basic Technology Textbook for JSS 1
- **Instructional Materials:** Charts showing technological tools, pictures of transportation and communication devices, short video clip on technology in daily life
- **Entry Behaviour:** Pupils have seen different tools and machines used at home and school.
- **Behavioural Objectives:** By the end of the lesson, pupils should be able to:
  1. Define technology in simple terms.
  2. State at least three importance of technology in everyday life.
  3. List three major branches of technology studied in basic technology.
  4. Match technological tools to the branches they belong to during class activities.
  5. Explain how technology makes life easier using personal experiences.

### Lesson Presentation

**Step 1 – Introduction:** Teacher displays different technological devices and asks pupils how these items make work easier.

**Step 2 – Presentation:** Teacher defines technology as the use of knowledge, tools, and skills to solve problems and improve life.

**Step 3 – Explanation:** Teacher discusses the importance of technology such as easing work, improving communication, and enhancing transportation, while pupils take notes.

**Step 4 – Application:** Pupils work in groups to sort pictures of technological devices into branches like communication, transportation, and production, then share their findings.

**Step 5 – Evaluation:** Teacher asks pupils to define technology, state two importance, and mention two branches, checking individual responses.

### Content Summary

1. Technology is the application of knowledge, skills, and tools to solve problems and make life easier.
2. Importance: saves time and energy, improves communication, increases production, aids learning.
3. Branches: communication, transportation, building, manufacturing, information and communication technology (ICT).

### Conclusion

Teacher revises the meaning, importance, and branches of technology and encourages pupils to observe technology around them.

### Assignment

List five technological devices you use at home or school and identify the branch of technology each belongs to.


DETAILED LESSON PLAN – JSS 2
-----------------------------

- **Date:** Wednesday, 17th September 2025
- **Class:** JSS 2
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Period:** Second
- **Topic:** First Aid
- **Subtopic:** Meaning of First Aid and Materials in a First Aid Box
- **Reference Materials:** NERDC Basic Science and Technology for JSS 2; MAN Basic Technology Textbook for JSS 2
- **Instructional Materials:** First aid box, bandages, cotton wool, adhesive plaster, antiseptic solution, triangular bandage
- **Entry Behaviour:** Students have witnessed minor injuries at home or school and know they require quick care.
- **Behavioural Objectives:** By the end of the lesson, students should be able to:
  1. Define first aid correctly.
  2. State the aims of giving first aid in emergency situations.
  3. Identify at least six materials found in a standard first aid box.
  4. Demonstrate how to arrange items properly in a first aid box.
  5. Explain how selected first aid materials are used in treating minor injuries.

### Lesson Presentation

**Step 1 – Introduction:** Teacher narrates a short story of a pupil who had a minor injury during play and asks students how the injury should be handled immediately.

**Step 2 – Presentation:** Teacher defines first aid as the immediate care given to an injured or sick person before professional medical help arrives.

**Step 3 – Explanation:** Teacher lists the aims of first aid such as preserving life, preventing the condition from worsening, and promoting recovery.

**Step 4 – Application:** Students examine a real or improvised first aid box, identify the materials inside, and discuss their uses in groups.

**Step 5 – Evaluation:** Teacher asks students to define first aid, state two aims, and mention four materials in the first aid box and their uses.

### Content Summary

1. First aid is the first help given to an injured or suddenly ill person before medical assistance.
2. Aims: save life, prevent further harm, encourage recovery.
3. Materials: bandages, cotton wool, antiseptic, adhesive plaster, scissors, safety pins, gloves, face mask, torchlight.

### Conclusion

Teacher summarises the meaning and aims of first aid and reinforces the proper use of first aid materials.

### Assignment

Draw and label a simple first aid box showing at least eight materials inside it.


DETAILED LESSON PLAN – JSS 3
-----------------------------

- **Date:** Friday, 19th September 2025
- **Class:** JSS 3
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Period:** Third
- **Topic:** Processing of Materials (Timber)
- **Subtopic:** Timber Defects, Treatments, and Presentation
- **Reference Materials:** NERDC Basic Technology for JSS 3; STAN Basic Technology Textbook for Junior Secondary Schools Book 3
- **Instructional Materials:** Samples or pictures of timber defects, timber treatment chemicals, sandpaper, varnish, timber finishing tools
- **Entry Behaviour:** Students have seen wooden furniture and are aware that some wood may have defects.
- **Behavioural Objectives:** By the end of the lesson, students should be able to:
  1. Identify common defects found in timber.
  2. Explain the causes of common timber defects.
  3. Describe basic treatments used to protect timber from defects.
  4. Outline the steps involved in presenting finished timber products.
  5. Recommend suitable treatments and presentation methods for different timber products.

### Lesson Presentation

**Step 1 – Introduction:** Teacher displays pieces of timber showing defects and asks students to describe what they observe.

**Step 2 – Presentation:** Teacher explains common timber defects such as warping, splitting, knots, insect attack, and fungal decay.

**Step 3 – Explanation:** Teacher discusses treatments like seasoning, chemical preservatives, painting, and varnishing while students note key points.

**Step 4 – Application:** Students, in groups, match types of defects to the correct treatments and suggest how finished products should be presented.

**Step 5 – Evaluation:** Teacher assesses students through oral questions on timber defects, treatments, and ways to present finished timber products.

### Content Summary

1. Common timber defects include knots, shakes, warping, insect attack, and fungal decay.
2. Treatments: proper seasoning, kiln drying, application of preservatives, painting, and varnishing.
3. Presentation: smoothing with sandpaper, filling holes, applying sealers, painting or varnishing, proper packaging and storage.

### Conclusion

Teacher reviews the identified defects, treatments, and presentation methods and encourages students to care for wooden materials.

### Assignment

Explain how you would prepare and present a wooden stool for sale, highlighting defects to check for and treatments to apply.

SECOND SEMESTER LESSON PLAN – JSS3 BASIC SCIENCE (WEEKS 1–8)

WEEK 1
Date: __________________
Class: JSS3 (Basic 9)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 1st Period
Topic: Ecology
Subtopic: Components of ecosystem
Reference Materials:
- NERDC Basic Science Curriculum
Instructional Materials:
- Ecosystem chart
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners have studied living/non-living things.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners can identify plants and animals.
Behavioural Objectives:
- Define ecosystem.
- Distinguish biotic and abiotic factors.
Lesson Presentations:
- Teacher introduces local ecosystem examples.
Lesson Content:
- Ecosystem is interaction of organisms with environment.
- Biotic: plants, animals, microbes; Abiotic: soil, water, light.
Evaluation:
- Define ecosystem.
Conclusion:
- Ecosystems contain living and non-living components.
Assignment:
- List five biotic and five abiotic factors in your school environment.

WEEK 2
Date: __________________
Class: JSS3 (Basic 9)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 2nd Period
Topic: Food Chain and Food Web
Subtopic: Energy transfer in ecosystem
Reference Materials:
- Approved Basic Science textbook
Instructional Materials:
- Food chain cards
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners know producers and consumers.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners can mention herbivores and carnivores.
Behavioural Objectives:
- Construct a food chain.
- Explain food web.
Lesson Presentations:
- Teacher demonstrates chain examples.
Lesson Content:
- Energy flows from sun to producers to consumers.
Diagram:
Sun -> Grass -> Grasshopper -> Frog -> Snake -> Hawk
Evaluation:
- Construct one food chain from your locality.
Conclusion:
- Food web is made of interconnected food chains.
Assignment:
- Draw one food web with at least four organisms.

WEEK 3

Subtopic: Male and female reproductive organs
Reference Materials:
- NERDC Curriculum
Instructional Materials:

Previous Knowledge:
- Learners know puberty changes.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners can state signs of adolescence.
Behavioural Objectives:

WEEK 4
Date: __________________
Class: JSS3 (Basic 9)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 4th Period
Topic: Heredity
Subtopic: Inherited traits and variation
Reference Materials:
- Approved textbook
Instructional Materials:
- Trait cards (eye colour, tongue rolling)
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners know family resemblance.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners compare visible traits.
Behavioural Objectives:
- Define heredity and variation.
- Give examples of inherited traits.
Lesson Presentations:
- Teacher leads trait observation activity.
Lesson Content:
- Heredity is transmission of traits from parents.
- Variation is difference among individuals of same species.
Evaluation:
- State three inherited traits.
Conclusion:
- Heredity and variation explain similarities and differences.
Assignment:
- Record three traits shared with your parents.

WEEK 5
Date: __________________
Class: JSS3 (Basic 9)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 5th Period
Topic: Simple Machines
Subtopic: Levers and pulleys
Reference Materials:
- NERDC Curriculum
Instructional Materials:
- Seesaw model, rope pulley image
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners have used household tools.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners identify tools that reduce effort.
Behavioural Objectives:
- Define simple machine.
- Explain mechanical advantage of levers and pulleys.
Lesson Presentations:
- Teacher demonstrates load-effort relationship.
Lesson Content:
- Simple machines make work easier by changing force.
Diagram:
Effort ----^---- Load
         Fulcrum
Evaluation:
- What is a fulcrum?
Conclusion:
- Machines multiply or redirect effort.
Assignment:
- List four examples of simple machines used at home.

WEEK 6
Date: __________________
Class: JSS3 (Basic 9)
Duration: 40 minutes
Period: 6th Period
Topic: Light Energy
Subtopic: Reflection and refraction
Reference Materials:
- Basic Science textbook
Instructional Materials:
- Mirror, glass cup and pencil
Previous Knowledge:
- Learners know light helps vision.
Entry Behaviour:
- Learners have seen reflection in mirrors.
Behavioural Objectives:
- Define reflection and refraction.
- State uses of mirrors and lenses.
Lesson Presentations:
- Teacher demonstrates reflected and bent rays.
Lesson Content:
- Reflection is bouncing back of light.
- Refraction is bending of light in different media.
Diagram:
Incident ray \
              \  | normal
---------------\-|------------- mirror
                / Reflected ray
Evaluation:
- Differentiate reflection from refraction.
Conclusion:
- Light changes direction by reflection and refraction.
Assignment:
- Give two practical uses each of mirrors and lenses.
